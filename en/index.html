My Letters

expressions of an idealist

1958 — 2025

Silvano Correa

"To dream for dreaming's sake is madness, is fantasy. To dream in order to shape those dreams into great and beautiful works, this is wisdom, is creation, is reality."

The hundreds of letters reproduced in these books, published in newspapers and magazines over more than six decades, represent the formation and thoughts of an idealist. They are reflections of an intense, varied life always guided by study, curiosity, and the exploration of new paths.

Highlight

Letter from President John F. Kennedy

United States Senate, Washington, D.C.

In January 1961, then president-elect John F. Kennedy sent this personal letter to Silvano Correa, thanking him for the congratulatory message on his election to the presidency of the United States.

Dear Mr. Correa:

I want to thank you for the very friendly message you sent to me after my election to the presidency.

I am most heartened by the many expressions of good will which I have received from abroad. I am sure that they reflect a broad unity of purpose throughout the world community. I hope that my record during the next four years will sustain your confidence.

With all good wishes, I am

Sincerely,
John F. Kennedy

January 13, 1961 — Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

About the Author

Silvano Correa was born in São Paulo in February 1939. He graduated in Economics and Mathematics from Duquesne University (Pittsburgh, USA) in 1962, living and studying in the United States for ten years, developing a critical Brazilian perspective on two cultures.

With a career in multinational companies and entrepreneurship, he founded Micro-Total Sistemas in 1980. A black belt in Aikido and student of Kardecist Spiritism, he dedicates his life to study, reflection, and active participation in society through his letters.
